Haven SSH Client - Privacy Policy

Last updated: 7 March 2026

Overview

Haven is an open-source SSH client for Android. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data Haven handles and how.

Data Collection

Haven does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data, usage analytics, or telemetry. No data is sent to us or any third party.

Data Stored on Your Device

Haven stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Connection profiles — hostnames, ports, usernames, and display labels you configure.
  • SSH keys — private keys generated or imported by you, stored in the app's private storage.
  • Preferences — settings such as font size, color scheme, and session manager choice.

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly initiate an SSH connection to a remote host.

Network Connections

Haven connects only to the SSH servers you configure. All connections use the SSH protocol, which is encrypted end-to-end. Haven does not contact any other servers.

Third-Party Services

Haven does not integrate with any advertising, analytics, or tracking services.

Open Source

Haven is open source under the MIT License. You can review the full source code at github.com/GlassOnTin/Haven.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this URL. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes.

Contact

If you have questions about this policy, please open an issue at github.com/GlassOnTin/Haven/issues.
